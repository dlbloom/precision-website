TILLAMOOK FOREST INTERPRETIVE CENTER

Recently, Precision Construction completed perhaps the most unique and challenging project in its history--construction of the Tillamook Forest Center, an interpretive center dedicated to showcasing the cultural and economic history of the Tillamook Forest.

  
   
 

Throughout all the work the Precision Construction team went to great lengths to protect the forest ecosystem. “It’s the biggest job we’ve ever done, and one of the toughest” said Precision Construction president Troy Weller. “Some of the biggest challenges came from the remote location. It took four months to get power and phones to the site.”

In addition to building the museum and education center, Precision Construction completed approximately 40 acres of landscape, a 40 foot replica of a fire lookout tower and a suspension bridge over nearby Jones Creek that proved to be a unique challenge. Precision Construction crews built the bridge in seven pieces in the parking lot and used the largest mobile crane in Oregon to set the pieces, all from one side of the river.

Frank Evans, Project Manager for the Oregon Department of Forestry, praised Precision Construction’s ability to deliver such a demanding, one-of-a-kind project. “This one,” he said “was literally carved out of a hand-planted forest.”
